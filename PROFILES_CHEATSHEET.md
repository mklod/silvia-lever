# Profiles Cheat Sheet

Plain-language guide to the brew profiles. Pick one with the **PROF** button,
make sure **BREW** says **AUTO**, tap to start. Grind notes matter — lower
pressure profiles need a coarser grind or they run slow.

---

## What the words mean

- **Preinfuse / bloom** — a gentle first soak at low pressure that wets the
  puck evenly before the real pressure hits. Like blooming pour-over coffee.
  Prevents the water from blasting channels through dry grounds.
- **Ramp** — how fast pressure climbs to its peak. Slow ramp = gentler, more
  even. Fast ramp = punchier.
- **Hold** — sitting at a steady pressure while the shot pulls.
- **Taper / decline** — easing pressure back down near the end for a softer
  finish and to avoid bitter over-extraction.
- **9 bar** — classic espresso pressure. **6 bar** — softer, sweeter, less
  channeling. **Lower pressure = slower flow** for the same grind.

---

## The profiles

### ☕ Standard 9-bar
**For:** everyday espresso, medium and dark roasts.
**Does:** quick gentle wet, then climbs to a steady 9 bar and holds.
**Aim:** classic balanced shot — body, crema, the usual.
**Grind:** your normal espresso grind (~1:2 in 25–35 s).

### 🍯 Gentle & Sweet
**For:** light-to-medium roasts, anyone chasing sweetness over punch.
**Does:** gentle wet, then holds a softer **6 bar** the whole way.
**Aim:** rounder, sweeter, less harsh. Lower pressure = less channeling on
roasts with fragile pucks.
**Grind:** **coarser than standard** — 6 bar flows slower, so a fine grind
will choke it. If the shot crawls, grind coarser.

### 🌸 Blooming Allongé
**For:** very light / nordic / fruity roasts where you want the bright
fruit and floral notes to sing.
**Does:** fast fill → **bloom** (pressure drops and the puck soaks) →
moderate 6 bar push → slow taper down to the end.
**Aim:** a long, thin, tea-like, super-clean cup. Maximum clarity — this is
the one that pulls fruity notes out of light roasts.
**Grind:** **fine**, long ratio (1:3 and up). Expect a longer, lighter-
bodied shot. Think "filter coffee in espresso form."

### 🌺 Blooming Espresso
**For:** light-to-medium roasts when you want clarity *and* some body.
**Does:** fill → bloom drop to 2 bar → ramps up to a full 9 bar and holds.
**Aim:** a bloom for evenness, then full pressure for richness. More texture
than the Allongé, still cleaner than Standard.
**Grind:** medium-fine, ~1:2 to 1:2.5.

### 🫖 Allongé
**For:** light roasts that channel or taste harsh on the blooming profiles.
**Does:** gentle wet, then a flat, easy **5 bar** hold.
**Aim:** simple, forgiving, high-clarity long shot. The fallback when
fancier profiles fight you.
**Grind:** **coarser**, long ratio (1:3–1:5), let it run ~30 s.

## Quick picker

| You want… | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Normal espresso, dark/medium | **Standard 9-bar** |
| Sweeter, softer, less bitter | **Gentle & Sweet** |
| Fruity light roast, max clarity | **Blooming Allongé** |
| Light roast with some body | **Blooming Espresso** |
| Light roast that keeps channeling | **Allongé** |

---

## Tips

- **Shot running too slow?** Grind coarser. Low-pressure profiles (6 bar and
  under) need a coarser grind than 9-bar espresso.
- **Shot gushing / sour?** Grind finer, or move to a blooming profile.
- **Take over by hand anytime** — turn the pump knob during a shot and the
  machine hands control to you smoothly (taper the end yourself).
- **Bloom/preinfuse** automatically ends after the first drops hit the cup,
  or after ~10 seconds if nothing drips (choked puck) — it won't sit forever.
- **Manual prime:** the machine is normally always primed. If it's been
  sitting dry, run a **FLUSH** first.
