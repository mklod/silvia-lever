revise Valve/water-flow logic. all valves are 3-way directional control valves, so there is a complex routing of water flow: 
 
  
 VALVE1 = VALVE_PUMP
	 open/energized: flow from pump to thermoblock OPV
	 closed/de energized: flow from pump to boiler OPV
  
 VALVE2 = VALVE_THERMOBLOCK
	 energized: flow from thermoblock coil to grouphead
	 de energized: flow from thermoblock coil to drain
 
 
to fill/prime the thermoblock: 
open/energize valve1, keep valve2 closed/de energized. pump/prime, visually inspect overflow coming out of drain. low pressure.

during brew (after priming and heating to brew temp):
open valve1,  pressing 'brew start' button immediately opens valve2 and allows pressure to build up at the grouphead while the motor is pumping. when 'brew stop' button is pressed, valve 2 is closed, pump is halted, and any remaining pressure is relieved through drain. max pressure is controlled by thermoblock OPV.

to fill/prime the steam boiler: 
close valve1. [valve2 is also closed by default, but irrelevant to steam boiler operation.] pump/prime, visually inspect overflow coming out of boiler OPV. 

during steam:
primed boiler is heated to set temp. water in full boiler has expanded to become steam. excess pressure relieved by OPV.
a mechanical steam valve (not connected to any electronics) is manually opened by the user and manually adjusted to output steam.
